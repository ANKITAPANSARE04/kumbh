Kumbh Mela Places

As we mentioned earlier, Kumbh Mela is celebrated in four major pilgrimages across India: Prayagraj, Ujjain, Haridwar, and Nashik. It is believed that Lord Vishnu released some drops of nectar from a pot (kumbh) at these places during Samudra Manthan. Saints, ascetics, sadhus, aspirants, and visitors gather in great numbers. At these places, sadhus and other pilgrims follow bathing rituals.

Significance of Kumbh Mela 2025

Kumbh Mela holds considerable significance as it is described in ancient Hindu texts and early Puranas. This year, Kumbh Mela 2024 started on 15th January and lasted till 8th March. Crores of pilgrims reached the place to celebrate the auspicious day. Four cities hosted the Kumbh Mela: Prayagraj at the Triveni Sangam in Haridwar on the banks of Ganga, Ujjain on the banks of holy Shipra, and Nashik on the banks of the Godavari river. Kumbh Mela is considered one of the world's largest festivals, attracting a significant number of pilgrims.

According to Hindu mythology, the festival is celebrated on a definite set of astrological positions. According to astrological calculations, the Kumbh festival takes place when Jupiter enters the Aquarius constellation along with the Sun and moves to the Aries constellation at that specific time. Millions of devotees dip in the holy river and get liberated from their past sins.

History of Kumbh Mela

The history behind Kumbh Mela is also a tremendous mythological story. From the beginning, Kumbh Mela has its roots in Samudra Manthan (churning of the ocean), an episode of Hinduism described in the Vishnu Purana. It is said that while performing the Samudra Manthan to win Nectar, Devas and Asuras constantly struggled.

At the command of Lord Vishu, Garuda took the Nectar to prevent it. In the process of occupying the Nectar, demons decided not to share it with gods, which led to 12 days of chase. Lord Vishnu released some drops of immoral nectar, which were believed to have been dropped in Prayagraj, Haridwar, Ujjain, and Nashik.

The first Kumbh Mela was celebrated around 644 BC, and this ritual was begun by King Harshvardhan in present-day Prayagraj.
